What the clean copy contains

The supplied clean copy is a research proposal and ends after Chapter 3. It defines the methodology and indicators for the future results, but it does not contain completed Chapters 4 or 5. This guide therefore follows that methodology without inventing participants, findings, significance tests, interview themes, or conclusions.

Evidence collection order

  1. Confirm approval and consent.Register only approved, consented participants and use participant codes in exports.
  2. Capture the baseline.Administer and record the pre-test before the intervention.
  3. Run the pilot consistently.Use the system for the agreed period while recording attempts, mastery, time, feedback and teacher interventions.
  4. Capture outcomes.Record the matching post-test and check that every comparison is paired to the same participant and learning outcome.
  5. Collect acceptance evidence.Administer the approved learner and teacher questionnaires; code interviews manually using the approved qualitative procedure.
  6. Check, export and analyse.Resolve missing or duplicate records, export the datasets, then apply only the statistical tests approved by your supervisor.

Chapter 4

Results and Analysis

  1. 4.1 Introduction — restate what evidence the chapter presents.
  2. 4.2 Participants — planned versus eligible sample, roles and completeness.
  3. 4.3 Pre/Post Results — paired descriptive results by learning outcome.
  4. 4.4 Learning Gain — mean, range, variance/standard deviation and approved gain calculation.
  5. 4.5 Mastery — attainment, attempts and time to mastery.
  6. 4.6 Feedback and Oversight — AI recommendations, teacher interventions, approvals and overrides.
  7. 4.7 User Acceptance — questionnaire summaries and manually verified qualitative themes.
  8. 4.8 Reliability and Summary — system use, synchronization incidents and a factual chapter summary.

Do not claim statistical significance unless the approved test, assumptions, sample and result are documented.

Chapter 5

Discussion and Conclusions

  1. 5.1 Introduction — explain how the discussion is organized.
  2. 5.2 Discussion by objective — finding, meaning, comparison with Chapter 2 literature, then the design-science contribution.
  3. 5.3 Implications — implications for CBC teachers, learners, schools and system design.
  4. 5.4 Limitations — sample, setting, duration, measurement and technical limitations.
  5. 5.5 Recommendations — evidence-based recommendations for practice and future work.
  6. 5.6 Conclusion — answer the research objectives without introducing new results.

Keep Chapter 4 factual. Interpret and compare those findings in Chapter 5.

Research integrity rules

  • Never use demo accounts or synthetic records as dissertation evidence.
  • Use participant codes, consent status and the approved study dates.
  • Leave a section as “{{ no_data }}” until genuine evidence exists.
  • Keep raw results separate from discussion and recommendations.
  • Ask your supervisor to approve the final statistical tests and qualitative coding procedure.
